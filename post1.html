3rd Underwater Clean-up Drive to Protect Capitancillo Islet Marine Sanctuary Conducted

September 7, 2024 City Government of Bogo

Bogo City remains steadfast in its commitment to preserving its rich marine biodiversity. The 3rd Underwater Clean-up Drive at the Capitancillo Islet Marine Sanctuary was successfully conducted through collaboration between the city government, national agencies, private individuals, groups, and volunteers.

The Capitancillo Islet, a designated marine protected area off the coast of Bogo City, is celebrated for its vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life. However, the sanctuary faces persistent threats such as marine debris, pollution, and coral predators like the crown-of-thorns starfish, necessitating consistent clean-up efforts to safeguard its ecosystem.

This initiative was spearheaded by the City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CENRO) and the City of Bogo Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (DRRMO) in partnership with local diving groups and passionate volunteers.

Event Highlights

  • Joint efforts by the city government, local stakeholders, and environmental advocates.
  • Removal of debris and coral predators threatening the marine sanctuary.
  • Participation of national agencies, private groups, and individual volunteers.
