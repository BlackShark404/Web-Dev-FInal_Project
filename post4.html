Bogo City Expands Livelihood Program for ReRe Graduates with Hydroponics Initiative

August 9, 2024 City Government of Bogo

In the expansion of the Rehabilitation and Reintegration Program (ReRe), Bogo City has introduced hydroponics to its “Umahan sa Kabag-uhan” (Farm of Change) initiative, providing former drug dependents with innovative opportunities to enhance their livelihoods.

The ReRe program, designed to support the rehabilitation and reintegration of individuals recovering from drug dependency, has been instrumental in helping participants reintegrate into society as productive community members. The “Umahan sa Kabag-uhan,” established at the City’s Bahay Silangan, has been a cornerstone of this effort, allowing graduates to cultivate a vegetable garden that produces weekly harvests sold at local markets. The proceeds from these sales are shared among the participants, providing them with a source of income.

Building on the success of this initiative, Bogo City is now incorporating hydroponics—a soil-free method of growing plants using nutrient-rich water—into the program. This advancement is expected to significantly boost agricultural productivity and efficiency, offering graduates a modern and sustainable approach to farming.

The hydroponics system, which has been introduced in partnership with the City Government and the Local Law Enforcement through the Bogo City Police Station, is anticipated to enhance the yield and quality of vegetable produce. This initiative aligns with the city’s broader goals of promoting sustainable agriculture and economic self-sufficiency among its residents.

Event Highlights

  • Expert-led discussions on livestock insurance and its benefits for raisers.
  • Introduction to innovative biosecurity measures to combat livestock diseases.
  • Demonstrations of new disinfectants and feed enhancers to improve productivity.
  • Engaging Q&A sessions with local farmers and industry professionals.

The forum underscored the city's commitment to empowering local livestock raisers through education and access to modern farming techniques. By providing these opportunities, the City of Bogo aims to enhance the livelihood and sustainability of its agricultural community.
